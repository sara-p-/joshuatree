Tuesday, September 28th

Our first time in a desert. Ever.

The first morning.

I woke up around 9am.

We had landed and driven in at night, and had no idea what the area actually looked like.

When I looked our the window I saw a gorgeous landscape of desert plants, surrounded on all sides by stunning mountains.

We took our coffee out to the porch, and spent the morning watching hummingbirds and little lizards dart across the yard. At one point we saw a roadrunner buzzing around in the distance. The weather was in the 70’s with a nice breeze.

We decided that we would go into the Park (Joshua Tree National Park) to watch the sunset, and we picked the Skull Rock Nature Trail as our destination.

Sunset at Skull Rock

Our first drive into the park

The drive through the park was wild. Not only was the geology/flora totally different than anything we’d ever seen, but it kept radically changing depending on the altitude.

Skull Rock is part of an area covered in massive boulders and hills, with desert plants growing out of every crevice and crack.

We got out of the car and were shocked at how nice the temperature was. The trail was a sandy trench that meandered around the boulders and outcroppings.

The trail was easy to follow at first, but then just seemed to dissolve into the rocks and we ended up just wandering through the boulders. At one point we climbed up an outcropping and saw tons of cairns everywhere. I cannot fully verbalize how enchanted we were by everything in the landscape. It was magnificent and so entirely different than anything either of us had seen before. By this point the sun was setting and the colors of everything around us were changing too.

Home again

Mushroom risotto and pan-seared chicken

After the sun set, it started getting dark pretty quickly, and we decided to drive back to our little desert home. We had the windows down so we could feel the cool breeze, and our favorite playlist playing. This was the first day of our trip, and it was already amazing. Our ears popped as we drove back to our Airbnb, and we realized that the Park was at a completely different altitude than our Airbnb.

When we got back we had a beer on the porch, and then started cooking dinner. Our Airbnb was surprisingly well-equipped, and we were able to cook mushroom risotto with pan-seared chicken. It was an unbelievably long process, but we were stoned and didn’t really mind. The end result was fucking delicious, and totally worth it.

We ended the day in the hot tub, and fell asleep around midnight.
